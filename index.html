Why Chobe?

Chobe, the land of of the giants

Did you know that Chobe is known as the "Land of the Giants" because it has the highest concentration of elephants in Africa? You can see up to 120,000 elephants at the Chobe National Park! The Chobe River is also unique; during the wet season, it flows both upstream and downstream, a rare phenomenon known as a "bifurcation." Chobe is also one of the few places where you can see the elusive puku antelope, which is rarely found outside the region.

The park's rich ecosystems support a wide range of wildlife, including lions, leopards, buffalo, and a variety of antelope species. The Chobe River is a lifeline for the park, attracting countless animals to its banks, especially during the dry season. Birdwatchers will also find Chobe a paradise, with over 450 bird species recorded in the area. Whether on a game drive or a river safari, Chobe National Park provides unforgettable opportunities to experience the beauty and diversity of African wildlife.
